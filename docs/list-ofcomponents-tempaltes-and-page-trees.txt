TUT USA WEBSITE DEFINITION
==========================

Website description
TUT USA is the primary digital experience for a luxury automotive brand focused on vehicle discovery, premium product storytelling, innovation education, ownership support, and high-touch conversion journeys. The site must help shoppers explore and compare vehicles, understand advanced engineering and design features, stay current with brand news, and access trusted technical information after purchase. It should feel premium, credible, calm, and precise, with strong support for dealer contact, test-drive requests, offers, manuals, service content, and owner education.

Main style guidelines for the designer
1. Premium minimalism: Use a restrained, elegant visual system with generous spacing, strong alignment, and deliberate composition. Avoid clutter and excessive promotional noise.
2. Automotive-first storytelling: Prioritize the car as the hero. Use large-format imagery, cinematic video, technical close-ups, and confident typography to communicate craftsmanship and performance.
3. Luxurious but usable: Interfaces should feel exclusive without becoming obscure. Navigation, CTAs, specs, comparison tools, and owner documentation must remain easy to understand and fast to use.
4. Precision and trust: Technical content, pricing language, ownership information, and regulatory details should appear structured, accurate, and dependable.
5. Dark-light contrast discipline: Favor sophisticated neutral palettes, high contrast, and carefully controlled accent usage. Accent colors should be used for emphasis, not decoration.
6. Editorial hierarchy: Headlines should be short and aspirational; body copy should be concise and informative; supporting labels should help users scan quickly.
7. Motion with restraint: Use animation to reinforce premium feel, reveal vehicle details, and guide attention. Avoid decorative motion that competes with product content.
8. Image and media standards: Visual assets should feel high-end, consistent, and realistic. Favor full-bleed hero imagery, polished studio photography, technical detail shots, and premium lifestyle scenes.
9. Conversion design: Key actions such as Build, Compare, Book a Test Drive, Find a Dealer, Request a Quote, and Sign In should be highly visible but never aggressive.
10. Ownership clarity: Manuals, service content, FAQs, and how-to material should prioritize readability, searchability, and structured navigation over brand theater.

TEMPLATE-LEVEL NOTES FOR PLATFORM TEAMS
--------------------------------------
- Use the Global Home Page, Model Overview, Model Detail, Innovation Hub, News Landing, Owners Hub, Learning Hub, and Contact / Concierge templates as the primary top-level template families.
- Reuse shared structural components across templates for consistency: Page Metadata, Breadcrumb, Primary Navigation, Footer, Search Bar, Filter Panel, Sort Control, CTA Button, FAQ, Related Content, and Contact Card.
- Restrict commerce-heavy components to retail-oriented templates only. For a luxury automotive brand, most vehicle journeys should lead to dealer contact, reservation, quote, or appointment rather than direct checkout.
- Keep knowledge and owner documentation templates largely free of campaign components so content remains trustworthy, scannable, and service-oriented.
- Keep innovation pages media-rich but technically disciplined: combine Feature List, Fact Box, Table / Data Table, Methodology Note, Fact Sheet, and related downloads.
- Allow platform admins to localize dealer, legal, financing, and owner-support content by region without changing the base template logic.
