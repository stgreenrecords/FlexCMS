Maximizing Range in Winter

Cold temperatures can impact battery efficiency. Follow our engineered protocols to maintain peak performance and preserve range during the winter months.

ac_unit Efficiency Priority: High
schedule Completion Time: 10 Mins

Operational Protocol

01

Pre-Conditioning the Battery

Before your departure, use the TUT app to start pre-conditioning while the vehicle is still plugged in. This draws energy from the grid rather than your battery to warm the cells to optimal operating temperature.

Technical Insight

Chemical reactions within the battery slow down in cold weather; pre-heating restores ions' mobility for full power delivery.

02

Utilize Targeted Heating

Heating the entire cabin via the HVAC system is energy-intensive. Prioritize heated seats and the steering wheel, which transfer heat directly to the occupants with significantly lower power consumption.

chair Seat Heating
settings_input_component Wheel Heating
03

Navigating to Superchargers

When planning a long journey, always use the in-vehicle navigation to select your charging stop. The vehicle will automatically begin "pre-conditioning for fast charging" as you approach, ensuring the battery is at the exact temperature required for maximum intake speed.

Frequently Asked

Why does my range estimate change?

The algorithm accounts for ambient temperature and historical driving data to give you a "real-world" projection, often appearing lower in winter to ensure accuracy.

Is regenerative braking affected?

Yes. A cold battery cannot accept high energy bursts. Regen may be limited until the battery warms up. Look for the 'Limited Regen' icon on your display.

What is the optimal tire pressure?

Pressure drops in cold air. Check your tires monthly during winter to maintain the recommended PSI for minimal rolling resistance and maximum range.
